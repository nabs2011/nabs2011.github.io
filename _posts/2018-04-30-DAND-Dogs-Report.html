Standardising the numerator to equal 14 for any values above 14 has resulted in a more normally distributed data set with mean's for all breeds more tightly centred around the average mean numerator rating of 11.14. Prior to standardising outliers:

1) Chows were the leader in ratings with an average of 14 
2) Labrador retrievers were second at 13.5

After standardising:

1) Samoyeds are most highly rated with an average of 11.71
2 Golden retrievers are second with an average of 11.51

Furthermore, when deep diving into the dog types, it's clear that Puppo's have the highest average rating for any dog type. Although interestingly, puppo's also do not have a upper tail, due to the smaller number of entries for puppos. Essentially this means that all puppos get a very strong rating.
